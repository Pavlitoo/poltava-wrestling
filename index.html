About the Wrestling

Freestyle wrestling is a combative sport and an Olympic sport consisting of a duel between two athletes according to certain rules, using various techniques (grabs, throws, coups, sweeps, etc.), in which each of the opponents tries to put the other on the shoulder blades, or gain a 10-point lead and win. In freestyle wrestling, unlike Greco-Roman and belt wrestling, it is allowed to grab the opponent's legs, sweep and actively use the legs when performing any technique. Modern freestyle wrestling is an Olympic and amateur sport.

History

Modern freestyle wrestling, according to UWW (formerly FILA), has been said to have originated in Great Britain and the United States by the name of "catch-as-catch-can" wrestling."Catch-as-catch-can" wrestling had a particular following in Great Britain and the variant developed in Lancashire had a particular effect on freestyle wrestling. "Catch-as-catch-can" wrestling gained great popularity in fairs and festivals during the 19th century. In catch-as-catch-can wrestling, both contestants started out standing and then a wrestler sought to hold his opponent's shoulder to the ground (known as a fall).
